Backtesting Engine Guide
Test 40 strategies against historical data
✕ Close
Before risking real money, test your strategy against actual historical data. The engine runs your chosen strategy on any NSE stock over any time period, simulating trades with realistic slippage, and produces a comprehensive performance report with 15+ risk metrics.
📌 How to Use — Step by Step
Step 1: Enter a stock symbol (e.g., RELIANCE, TCS, INFY).
Step 2: Select a strategy — 39 options across Technical (SMA Crossover, MACD, Supertrend, Bollinger, Breakout), Value (Low P/E, Deep Value, GARP), Quality (Piotroski, High ROE, Low Debt), Growth (EPS Growth, Revenue), and Hybrid (Growth+Breakout, Quality+MACD).
Step 3: Set time period (start/end dates) and initial capital.
Step 4: Configure exits — Stop Loss %, Target %, and advanced exits (ATR trailing, time-based, R-multiple).
Step 5: Click RUN BACKTEST. View equity curve, trade log, and risk metrics.
📈 Equity Curve
Visual chart showing how capital grows over time. Steadily rising with shallow drawdowns = good strategy. Compared against buy-and-hold benchmark.
📊 Key Metrics
Total Return, CAGR, Win Rate, Sharpe Ratio (above 1.0 is good), Max Drawdown, Sortino Ratio, Calmar Ratio, Avg Win vs Avg Loss, Profit Factor.
Investors: Validate strategy before deploying capital. Test across market cycles to understand drawdown risk.  |  Advisors: Evidence-backed recommendations — "this strategy returned 24% CAGR with 1.3 Sharpe" carries weight with clients.

The more strategies that agree on a stock, the higher the conviction score.
📌 How to Use

BACKTESTING ENGINE
Test 40 strategies against historical data
Before risking real money, test your strategy against actual historical data. The Backtest Engine runs your chosen strategy on any NSE stock over any time period, simulating actual trades with realistic slippage, and produces a comprehensive performance report.
📌 How to Use
Step 1: Enter a stock symbol (e.g., RELIANCE, TCS, INFY).
Step 2: Select a strategy — 39 options across Technical (SMA Crossover, MACD, Supertrend, Bollinger, Breakout), Value (Low P/E, Deep Value, GARP), Quality (Piotroski, High ROE, Low Debt), Growth (EPS Growth, Revenue), and Hybrid (Growth+Breakout, Quality+MACD).
Step 3: Set the time period (start/end dates) and initial capital.
Step 4: Configure exit parameters — Stop Loss %, Target %, and advanced exits (ATR trailing, time-based, R-multiple).
Step 5: Click RUN BACKTEST. Results show equity curve, trade log, and 15+ risk metrics.
📌 Understanding Results
📈 Equity Curve
Visual chart showing how your capital grows (or shrinks) over time. A steadily rising curve with shallow drawdowns = a good strategy. Compare against buy-and-hold benchmark shown alongside.
📊 Key Metrics
Total Return, CAGR, Win Rate, Sharpe Ratio (risk-adjusted returns — above 1.0 is good), Max Drawdown (worst peak-to-trough decline), Sortino Ratio, Calmar Ratio, Avg Win vs Avg Loss, Profit Factor.
🎯 Who benefits most?
Investors: Validate your strategy before deploying capital. Test across different market cycles to understand drawdown risk.  |  Advisors: Provide evidence-backed recommendations — "this strategy returned 24% CAGR over 5 years with a 1.3 Sharpe" carries weight with clients.

FORWARD TESTING
Run strategies on live market data automatically
Forward Testing bridges the gap between backtesting and real trading. It runs your chosen strategy on the live market every day, auto-scans the Nifty 50/100/500 universe, generates signals with strength scores, and tracks performance with real equity curves — all without you placing a single manual trade.
Create a Test: Name your test, select a strategy and stock universe (Nifty 50/100/500 or custom), set entry/exit rules, capital allocation, and sector caps.
Monitor: View active signals with strength scores, open positions with live P&L, and the trade log. The hit rate metric shows what percentage of generated signals were profitable.
Iterate: Run multiple forward tests with different strategies side by side. Compare hit rates and returns to find which strategies work best in current market conditions.
Investors: Real-time strategy validation — see if your backtest results hold up in live markets.  |  Advisors: Build a verified track record of strategy performance. Forward test results can back up your advisory recommendations with live data.
